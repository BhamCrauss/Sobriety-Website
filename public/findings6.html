Family Histories

This page is very interesting, but I want to note that it is beyond the scope of this project to definitively make a claim about the implications of an addict's or alcoholic's family history of addiction with their chances to recover.

That being said, the first graph shows that a respondent who has no family history of addiction or a history addiction only on their mother's side is twice as likely not to relapse after starting treatment or a program than respondents who have a family history of addiction on their father's side or both sides of their family.

Also, a respondent is 2 to 3 times more likely to relapse 4 or more times if they have a family history of addiction on their father's side of the family or both sides of the family.

The second graph on this page shows that despite a respondents family history of addiction, the prevalence of time in sobriety is more or less the same within the four catagories. This shows that despite a respondent's family history, recovery from alcoholism and addiction IS POSSIBLE and HAS BEEN DONE BEFORE!
