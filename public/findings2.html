A REALISTIC PICTURE OF SOBRIETY




What They Do

Whether you are a friend, family member or employer of an addict or alcolholic, having a realistic picture of what sobriety looks like and what it takes will be beneficial. One common misunderstanding about what it takes to recover from this disease is that it is a decision to be made once and then life will return to normal from there. Another is that if the addict or alcolholic relapses, espiecially more than once, that they will never 'get it'.

This survey shows, clearly, that recovery is an ongoing process that requires work and maintenance. In fact, the majority of respondents with over 10 years of clean time, still, go to meetings once a week. Also, the majority of our results show that relapse was a common obstacle faced by these recovering addicts and alcoholics. Relapse is disheartening and dangerous, but many people rebounded from these slip-ups.
